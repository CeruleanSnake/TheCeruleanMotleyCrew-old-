Welcome to The Cerulean Motley Crew!

This will have to be my biggest project I have ever taken up on. Would you also believe this was my first ever time with major coding too? It's been a great experiment and project and I have learnt so many skills, even despite my reboot from the Original version.
I honestly don't regret rebooting, I have a much better story structure, more fleshed out characters and the original served as a good training ground to get my style down.
This project has also given me something creative to work on. Being physically disabled it can be tough to actually get out and keep your mind active, so I guess you could say its kinda like my therapy, lol!

The original story was a concept I actually thought up many many years ago, probably 2012 or so. I never really did anything with it though. The first time I really started a MSPA-styled was with 3 other friends on Go Stuck Yourself in 2014 (I drew the Chaz Parts).
Like many team projects, interest died and it fell to the wayside. I never really did try again after that.

So how did I get started on this?
Years later, In 2020, I managed to catch up with a few old friends in the Homestuck scene. So I was chatting away with peepee swine and she was talking about how she throws in a bunch of trolls in the background of I'm Good, I'm Gone. I said "Damn I wish my troll was in a fanventure or some sort" and she said "Why not make one?" So I said "You know what, yeah! why not?"
So I sat down, dusted off my old pirate concept, polished it a bit and got to work. Within 20 or so days I had drawn up 30 panels and finally started publishing on MSPFA. Of course I got sick, saw lots of doctors and it fell to the backburner a bit.

It gave me some time to look over the story, realise what I wanted and where I was struggling and decided to reboot the comic a year later, so here we are today! Never been more prouder in any of my projects!

This commentary snippet has gone on way too long now, I promise the others wont be so long lol... maybe...
Anyway, enjoy reading! I've enjoyed drawing it!

-TheCeruleanSnake
